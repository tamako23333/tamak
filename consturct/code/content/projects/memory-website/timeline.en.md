<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>Childhood in the Lilong</h3><p>I was born in a lane house in Hongkou, Shanghai. It was early summer, and the gardenia flowers at the lane entrance were in full bloom. My father worked as an accountant at a textile mill; my mother taught at a neighborhood primary school. Every morning, the lane filled with the cries of street vendors — soy milk sellers, umbrella repairmen, knife sharpeners — my earliest memories. The lane was short, just five minutes, but held an entire world.</p></div>

<div class="timeline-year-marker">1949</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/vintage-school/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>A New School, A New Era</h3><p>At thirteen, I entered the No.1 Municipal Girls' School. An ancient ginkgo tree in the courtyard carpeted the ground in gold each autumn. It was there I first read Lu Xun, Bing Xin, and Ba Jin. My Chinese teacher, Miss Zhou, said my writing had "a spark." I remembered those words my whole life — and later became a Chinese teacher myself.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-panel panel-left">
<div class="timeline-year-marker">1958</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/classroom-blackboard/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>The First Lesson</h3><p>After graduating from teachers' college, I was assigned to a junior high school in Zhabei. Standing at the podium for the first time, my palms were drenched in sweat. Forty pairs of bright eyes stared back at me. The first essay I taught was Zhu Ziqing's "Retreating Figure." By the last paragraph, the classroom was perfectly still. A few girls had tears in their eyes. In that moment I knew: this was what I'd do for the rest of my life.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-panel panel-right">
<div class="timeline-year-marker">1966</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/tea-couple/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>Marriage · Storm</h3><p>That year I married Lao Chen. He was an engineer at the shipyard. The wedding was simple: two families sharing a meal, a new bed, and we became a family. The years that followed were hard, but we held each other up. Lao Chen was a man of few words, but every evening he made me a cup of tea and placed it at the corner of my desk. Marriage isn't grand declarations — it's a cup of tea, warmed again and again.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-panel panel-left">
<div class="timeline-year-marker">1979</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/graduation-class/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>Golden Years</h3><p>Everything began to bloom again. I returned to the podium I loved, full of energy. Those ten-plus years were the most fulfilling of my teaching career — I guided several graduating classes, my students won city-level awards, I was promoted to senior teacher. The classroom came alive again. Students dared to think, to speak, to write honestly. Those classes became tiny sparks of enlightenment.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-panel panel-right">
<div class="timeline-year-marker">1996</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/writing-journal/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>Retirement, Not Retreat</h3><p>On my last day, the students threw me a party. A boy stood up and said I made him fall in love with literature. I wept. Back home, Lao Chen handed me a fresh notebook and said, "Now you can write your own stories." Since then, I've filled over a dozen notebooks — poetry, memoirs, recipes. From teaching others to write, to becoming a writer myself — a beautiful full circle.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-panel panel-left">
<div class="timeline-year-marker">2008</div>
<div class="timeline-content-card">
<div class="timeline-card-img"><img src="https://picsum.photos/seed/europe-travel/800/600" alt="" loading="lazy"></div>
<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>The First Far Journey</h3><p>The year my granddaughter graduated, she took Lao Chen and me to Europe. In Paris, I stood beneath the Eiffel Tower for the first time — so much taller than I'd imagined. At the Trevi Fountain in Rome, we tossed coins together. We rode boats in Venice, saw David in Florence, gazed at the Alps. I wrote seventeen pages in my journal. To have a first time like this in your seventies — a gift.</p></div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="timeline-card-text"><h3>The Taste of Home</h3><p>My granddaughter helped compile all my recipes into a small book — handwritten pages with old family photos. We printed a hundred copies for family and friends. They wept as they read. It wasn't the recipes — it was the time, the stories, the home folded into every ingredient. I'm still writing every day. Words help me remember what shouldn't be forgotten, and show my children where they come from.</p></div>
